Here's what the team landed on:

**Core Idea: AI-Powered Personal Secretary / Email Assistant**

A personal AI secretary that monitors your inbox and helps you stay on top of things — essentially a "Jarvis for your email."

**Two Main Features**
1. **Chat with your mailbox** — ask natural language questions like "how many KrisFlyer points do I have?" or "what bills are due?" and it reads your emails to answer.
2. **Daily action item digest** — proactively surfaces things like unpaid bills, expiring passwords, unreplied emails, pending insurance claims, etc.

**Bonus Skills (stretch goals)**
- File organizer — moves downloaded files into the right project folders based on learned memory of your folder structure.
- Invoice/bill splitting — upload a receipt, split costs among people.
- Event/issue logging — log incoming reports or complaints to the right project.

**Interface**
- Primary: web UI or chat interface (Streamlit or similar), not just CLI.
- Claude Code as a possible developer-facing interface, but a proper UI is the priority for demo purposes.

**Personas to demo** (to show breadth)
- Developer — log analysis, project file organization
- Manager — email routing/forwarding to the right person
- Finance/HR — bill tracking, invoice splitting

**Practical decisions**
- Use a dummy Gmail account (avoid connecting to real/personal inboxes for the demo), leveraging Gmail MCP.
- Dropped the "agent evaluation" idea — too developer-niche, not broad enough.
- Slack as a potential extension to mention but not build.

**Dropped ideas:** Agent evaluation framework (too dev-specific, doesn't fit the "automate repetitive work for anyone" brief).
